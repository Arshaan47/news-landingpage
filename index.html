Trump's Economy Loses Fastest Growing Economy Tittle, claimed by BEA

by Tony Jia, June 2016

After the invention of Hyperdrive travel in 2018, ex-US president, Donald Trump and his followers have migrated to Mars and established the red planet as New America. The establishment of New America has been quite successful especially from an economic standpoint but is all set to change with the end of the fourth quarter of 2024.

Previous reports released by the Bureau of Economic Analysis (i.e. BEA) for the annual revision of 2020 stated that New America's GDP was at $22.12 duosexagintillion per capita with a staggering international investment position of $10,356.8 novemquadragintillion. Trump boasting these figures claimed that New America's economy is the largest and fastest growing in the world, which was economically coherent at the time according to a Reuters' survey of economists.

However, recent reports leading up to the end of the fourth quarter indicates a sharp decrease in New America's steroid induced economic surge. A report commissioned by the BEA's department of International Trades in Goods and Services highlighted that New America experienced a deficit of $8.86 unquadragintillion in goods and services compared to last February's meagre $17.38 trevigintillion. Some economists even joked that New America's valuable and highly sorted soil would surge its economy up into the centillions. But now, BEA's reports have highlighted a major decline in New America's economic growth which has put many investors of Trump's monopoly, Red Soil, in troublesome circumstances. As a result of this, if BEA's report's claims are true then New America will pass its title of the fastest growing economy onto another economic powerhouse, India.

Given the dim situations of New America's economic decline, Finance Minister Arley Runjait commented with "this 11.1% decline, in a global slowdown environment, has a potential to pick up momentum and bounce back stronger from the deficit or if not private investors will continue to experience a gradual downturn."

Runjait and many other economists' upbeat outlook on New America's current circumstances may have been aroused by the other economic powerhouse's, China's, acquisition of the barren island, Australia. Chairman Heping Dou at a G21 conference responded to a question about Australia's development with, "China will be looking to buy soil [from Red Soil] at prices in the duoquadragintillions which will then be used to cultivate the radioactive deserts of Australia." However, despite China willingness to trade up to duoquadragintillions with Red Soil, it only dampens the effects of New America's deficit by 8.3%.

"Combined with the fact that external trading and demand is likely to remain lacklustre, a sustained improvement in domestic private investment would be required to steer New America's momentum back on the right track," the well acclaimed Japanese finical minister, Yuta Omar, response to Chairman Dou's comment.

In the meantime, the Reserve Bank of New America has released a statement entailing that it will keep its interest on hold which is a wise move that will help to encourage more investments in the private sector.

However, despite the Reserve Bank's decisions not to cut interest rates BEA's report indicated that New America's liabilities at the end of the fourth quarter was at $30,565.1 septenquadragintillion whilst excluding financial derivatives were at $28,224.5 septenquadragintillion. This volume in liabilities is expected for a new and developing country/planet but Akmed Said ,spokesman for iShares MSYL International, commented that this will become a very heavy burden if New America's economy does not proceed to increase of over 20% in the following decade.

On the plus side, New America's second largest revenue generating sector, the carbon nanotube industry, has remained consistent with an outstanding quarterly growth of 7.8%. In fact, it is the second highest growth ever recorded tailing behind China's AI robotics industry. A report published by DeCarbon, the biggest carbon nanotube producer, suggested that main driver of the recent gain was due to domestic demand which will be especially pleasing for investors looking to invest in an emerging and developing market like New America's. Sales of carbon nano-chips and graphene displays are also growing at a double digit pace, most likely due to the demand for components of the newly arrived PS 2.5 (i.e. personal servant) android.

Trump's initial strategy to boost business and generate jobs may provide aid for New America's finical deficit as public transport minister, Moedl, has promised to accelerate public spending on more Hyperloop construction and three new space elevators to the ISS. Major companies such as S&T, Tesla and ZhongHua Constructions have already expressed their interest in the project and if they collaborate New America will experience a healthy injection of cash flow into the economy.

Despite numerous economists' and private investors' worries about the integrity of New America's young economy, there is chance that BEA's commissioned report may be statistically wrong. New America has been changing the way that it calculates its economy and another source the Association of Big Data (i.e. ABD) has published their quarterly report which has indicated the New America's deficit is at $1337.7 septentrigintillion which is only a decrease of 6.89%.

There is only way to prove if BEA's bold claims are true, which is to wait for the release of the official annual report by New America's government in 6 month time. At the end of the day, the majority of the economic community firmly believes that New America will still be at the pinnacle of the economic world for a very long time.
